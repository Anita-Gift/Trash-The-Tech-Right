Implications of Improper Disposal

The environmental and health consequences.

Environmental Impact

* Soil contamination: Toxic substances like lead, mercury and cadmium can leach into the soil which makes it infertile,harming plants and animals.

* Air Pollution: Burning e-waste produces hazardous byproducts like dioxins and furans, which are carcinogenic and toxic to humans and the environment.

* Water pollution: Chemicals from e-waste can seep into groundwater or be washed into rivers and oceans, contaminating drinking water and aquatic ecosystems

Health Hazards

* Open-air burning and acid baths release toxic substances like lead, mercury, cadmium, and arsenic, causing irreversible health effects such as cancers, miscarriages, neurological damage, and diminished IQs

* Toxic chemicals from e-waste can enter crops and livestock through contaminated soil and water, affecting communities consuming these products

Long-Term Consequences

The effects of improper e-waste disposal can persist for generations. It not only harms the environment but also undermines the health and well-being of communities.
